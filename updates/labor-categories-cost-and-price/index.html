Labor categories now set cost and price

Give each labor category a default cost and price per hour so quote labor items prefill with the right margin. A change log tracks every edit, and existing quotes keep the rates they were built with.

Labor categories used to be just names in a dropdown. Now each one carries the numbers your quotes actually need, so adding labor to a quote starts from the right cost and price instead of a blank line.

Cost and price on every category

In Labor Categories, each labor category now has three fields. The quote settings guide covers each one:

  • Labor Category name — for example CNC Labor, Shop Labor, Design Labor, or Installation.
  • Default cost / hr — what that labor costs your shop, used for margin.
  • Default price / hr — what you charge the customer.

When you add a labor item in the Quote Builder, it prefills with the cost and price you set here, so the margin is right from the start. Use Add Labor Category for a new row and Remove for one you no longer need.

Existing quotes keep their rates

Updating a category's name, cost, or price changes the defaults used for new quote line items only. Quotes that already include labor keep the cost and price they were created with, so historical quotes never shift under you.

A change log for your labor rates

A paginated change log sits below the labor categories. It records who changed a category, what changed (name, cost, or price), and when, so rate changes are easy to track over time.
